Go-to-Market Execution

Six Thinking Hats Product Launch Strategy

A comprehensive multi-perspective analysis to ensure ModuCore disrupts the conventional smartphone industry upon release.

White Hat

Facts & Info
  • • Define specific parameters of the launch: exact release date, target retail price, and initial shipment inventory (e.g., 50k Core units).
  • • Detail the exact hardware specs: UniBoard dimensions, SoC benchmarks, magnetic latch tension ratings.
  • • Map out distribution channels (Direct-to-Consumer via website, select premium carrier partnerships).

Red Hat

Emotions
  • • Cultivate a feeling of empowerment—"You finally own your hardware."
  • • Address aesthetic desire: The 3D neon-cyberpunk vibe makes the repairable phone desirable, not just practical.
  • • Gauge early impressions: The satisfying physical 'CLICK' of the magnetic snaps creates an addictive tactile emotional response.

Black Hat

Caution & Risk
  • • Durability concerns: Will dust/water ruin exposed pins? (Mitigation: IP68 rated gaskets on magnetic seals).
  • • Software ecosystem risks: Can Android handle hot-swapping camera modules without crashing?
  • • Supply chain bottlenecks: Dependency on third-party SoC vendors making compatible Compute Cards.

Yellow Hat

Optimism / Value
  • • Long-term ROI: Highlighting the 5-7 year lifespan of the UniBoard reduces perceived high entry cost.
  • • Environmental win: Massive reduction in e-waste by only replacing broken screens or outdated SoCs.
  • • The "Future-Proof" guarantee: Users buy once, upgrade infinitely. Highest consumer value proposition in the market.

Green Hat

Creativity / Ideas
  • • Developer kits: Launch an open-source schematic for the USB-Extension Dock to let modders build custom accessories (e.g., thermal cameras, macro rigs).
  • • "Aesthetics as a Module": Sell transparent, retro-gaming, or carbon fiber outer shells that snap magnetically.
  • • Gamified launch event: "Assemble your own phone" live interactive digital experiences (like this very website).

Blue Hat

Process Control
  • • Launch sequence: Teaser Phase (CGI renders) -> Influencer Phase (Hands-on assembly videos) -> Pre-Order Phase (Configurator live).
  • • Synthesize feedback: Use Black Hat concerns to write the primary FAQ page structure.
  • • Success Metrics tracking: Monitor modular component attachment rates vs core system purchases post-launch.
